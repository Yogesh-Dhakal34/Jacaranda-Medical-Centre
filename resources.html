WHAT TO EXPECT

Check-Up Guidelines

🩺

Before Your Visit

  • Fast for 8 hours if blood tests are required
  • Bring previous medical records or prescriptions
  • List any current medications you are taking
  • Arrive 15 minutes before your appointment
🏥

During Your Visit

  • A nurse will record your vitals first
  • Your doctor will review your medical history
  • Be honest about your symptoms — no detail is too small
  • Ask questions — our doctors are here to help
📋

After Your Visit

  • Follow the prescribed treatment plan carefully
  • Collect your prescriptions from our pharmacy
  • Schedule any recommended follow-up appointments
  • Call 1134 anytime for emergencies

CONTINUING YOUR CARE

Follow-Up Instructions

01

Take Medications as Prescribed

Never skip doses or stop medication early without consulting your doctor, even if you feel better.

02

Attend All Follow-Up Appointments

Follow-up visits allow your doctor to monitor your recovery and adjust treatment if necessary.

03

Watch for Warning Signs

Contact us immediately if you experience worsening symptoms, allergic reactions, or any new concerns.

04

Rest and Recovery

Give your body time to heal. Maintain a healthy diet, stay hydrated, and avoid strenuous activity unless cleared by your doctor.

KNOW YOUR HEALTH

Basic Health Education

Eat Well, Live Well

A balanced diet is the foundation of good health. Aim for a mix of proteins, carbohydrates, healthy fats, vitamins, and minerals in every meal. Drink at least 8 glasses of water daily, reduce processed food intake, and include fresh fruits and vegetables in every meal.

Hygiene Prevents Disease

Wash your hands thoroughly with soap for at least 20 seconds before eating, after using the toilet, and after coughing or sneezing. Keep wounds clean and covered. Maintain oral hygiene by brushing twice daily and visiting your dentist regularly.

Mental Health Matters

Mental health is just as important as physical health. Talk to someone you trust when feeling overwhelmed. Get 7–8 hours of sleep, exercise regularly, and limit screen time. If you are experiencing anxiety or depression, our specialists are here to help — there is no shame in seeking support.

Know When to Call Emergency

Call 1134 immediately if you or someone nearby experiences: chest pain or pressure, difficulty breathing, sudden severe headache, loss of consciousness, uncontrolled bleeding, or signs of stroke (face drooping, arm weakness, speech difficulty). Do not wait — every second counts.
